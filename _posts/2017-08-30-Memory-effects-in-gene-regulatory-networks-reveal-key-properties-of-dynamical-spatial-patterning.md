##### Abstract
Gene regulatory networks (GRNs) control cellular function and decision making during tissue development and homeostasis. Mathematical tools based on dynamical systems theory are often used to model these networks. The size and complexity of these models, however, mean that their behaviour is not always intuitive and the underlying mechanisms can be difficult to decipher. For this reason, methods that simplify and aid exploration of complex networks are necessary. To this end we develop a broadly applicable form of the Zwanzig-Mori projection. By first converting a thermodynamic state ensemble model of gene regulation into mass action reactions we derive a general method that produces a set of time evolution equations for a subset of components of a network. The influence of the rest of the network, the bulk, is captured by memory functions that describe how the subnetwork reacts to its own past state via components in the bulk. We demonstrate that the method captures the full dynamics of a simple cross-repressive transcriptional motif while simplifying analysis of the subnetwork and providing a natural interpretation of the memory functions. We then apply the approach to a GRN from the vertebrate neural tube, a well characterised developmental transcriptional network composed of four interacting transcription factors. This reveals the function of specific links within the neural tube network and identifies features of the regulatory structure that increase the robustness of the network to initial conditions. Taken together, the study provides evidence that Zwanzig-Mori projections offer powerful and effective tools for simplifying and exploring the behaviour of GRNs.

基因调控网络（GRN）控制组织发育和体内平衡过程中的细胞功能和决策。基于动力系统理论的数学工具经常被用来模拟这些网络。然而，这些模型的大小和复杂性意味着它们的行为并不总是直观的，并且底层机制可能难以破译。出于这个原因，简化和帮助复杂网络的探索的方法是必要的。为此，我们开发了Zwanzig-Mori投影的广泛适用形式。通过首先将基因调控的热力学状态集合模型转换为质量作用反应，我们推导出一种通用方法，该方法为网络组件的子集产生一组时间演化方程。网络其余部分的影响（大部分）由内存函数捕获，这些内存函数描述了子网络如何通过批量中的组件对其自身的过去状态做出反应。我们证明，该方法捕捉了一个简单的交叉压制转录主题的完整动态，同时简化子网的分析和提供记忆功能的自然解释。然后，我们将这种方法应用于脊椎动物神经管的一个GRN中，这个神经管是由四个相互作用的转录因子组成的一个特征良好的发育转录网络。这揭示了神经管网络中特定连接的功能，并确定了调节结构的特征，这增加了网络对初始条件的稳健性。综合起来，这项研究提供的证据表明，Zwanzig-Mori的预测为简化和探索GRNs的行为提供了强大和有效的工具。

##### Abstract
Today's landscape of robotics is dominated by vertical integration where single vendors develop the final product leading to slow progress, expensive products and customer lock-in. Opposite to this, an horizontal integration would result in a rapid development of cost-effective mass-market products with an additional consumer empowerment. The transition of an industry from vertical integration to horizontal integration is typically catalysed by de facto industry standards that enable a simplified and seamless integration of products. However, in robotics there is currently no leading candidate for a global plug-and-play standard. 
 This paper tackles the problem of incompatibility between robot components that hinder the reconfigurability and flexibility demanded by the robotics industry. Particularly, it presents a model to create plug-and-play robot hardware components. Rather than iteratively evolving previous ontologies, our proposed model answers the needs identified by the industry while facilitating interoperability, measurability and comparability of robotics technology. Our approach differs significantly with the ones presented before as it is hardware-oriented and establishes a clear set of actions towards the integration of this model in real environments and with real manufacturers.

当今的机器人技术是由垂直整合主导，单一厂商开发最终产品导致进展缓慢，昂贵的产品和客户锁定。与此相反，横向一体化将带来高成本效益的大众市场产品的快速发展，增加消费者的权力。一个行业从垂直一体化向横向一体化的转变，通常是通过事实上的行业标准促成的，从而实现产品的简化和无缝集成。然而，在机器人领域，目前还没有一个全球即插即用标准的主要候选者。
 本文解决了机器人组件之间不兼容的问题，这些问题阻碍了机器人行业所需的可重构性和灵活性。特别是它提供了一个创建即插即用机器人硬件组件的模型。我们提出的模型不是迭代地演化以前的本体，而是在满足行业确定的需求的同时，促进机器人技术的互操作性，可测性和可比性。我们的方法与之前提出的方法有很大的不同，因为它是面向硬件的，并且建立了一套清晰的行动来将这个模型在真实的环境中与真正的制造商进行整合。

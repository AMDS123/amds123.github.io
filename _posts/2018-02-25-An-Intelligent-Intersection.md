##### Abstract
Intersections are hazardous places. Threats arise from interactions among pedestrians, bicycles and vehicles, more complicated vehicle trajectories in the absence of lane markings, phases that prevent knowing who has the right of way, invisible vehicle approaches, vehicle obstructions, and illegal movements. These challenges are not fully addressed by the "road diet" and road redesign prescribed in Vision Zero plans. Nor will they be completely overcome by autonomous vehicles with their many on-board sensors and tireless attention to sensor readings. 
 Accidents can also occur because drivers, bicyclists and pedestrians do not have the information they need to avoid wrong decisions. In these cases, the missing information can be calculated and communicated by an intelligent intersection. The information gives the current full signal phase, an estimate of the time when the phase will change, the occupancy of the blind spots of the driver or autonomous vehicle, and detection of red-light violators. The paper develops a design of the intelligent intersection, motivated by the analysis of an accident at an intersection in Tempe, AZ, between an automated Uber Volvo and a manual Honda CRV. The intelligent intersection functions as a `protected intersection' using I2V communication.

交叉口是危险的地方。威胁来自行人，自行车和车辆之间的相互作用，没有车道标记的更复杂的车辆轨迹，妨碍知道谁拥有通行权的阶段，隐形车辆进近，车辆障碍物和非法移动。 Vision Zero计划中规定的“道路饮食”和道路重新设计并未充分解决这些挑战。它们也不会被具有许多车载传感器的自动驾驶车辆完全克服，并且不知疲倦地关注传感器读数。
 事故还可能发生，因为驾驶员，骑车者和行人不具备避免错误决定所需的信息。在这些情况下，可以通过智能交叉口来计算和传达缺失的信息。该信息给出了当前的完整信号阶段，阶段将改变的时间的估计，驾驶员或自主车辆的盲点的占用以及红灯违章者的检测。本文开发了一个智能交叉口的设计，这是通过分析在亚利桑那州坦佩市的一个交叉路口发生的事故，这个交叉口位于自动优步沃尔沃和手动本田CRV之间。智能交叉点作为使用I2V通信的“受保护交叉口”。

##### Abstract
Despite decades of studies, the mechanism that determines human locomotion is still unknown, available models and motor control theories can only partially capture the phenomenon. This may be is probably the principal cause of the reduced efficacy of lower limbs rehabilitation therapies. Recently, it has been proposed that human locomotion may be planned in the task-space by taking advantage of the gravitational pull acting on the Centre of Mass (CoM) that we have used to formulate a task-space planner for straight locomotion at a constant speed. The proposed model represents the CoM transversal trajectory as simple harmonic oscillator moving forward at a constant speed. On the other hand, the vertical trajectory of the CoM is controlled through the ankle strategies. Our solution is composed of closed-form equations which can plan human-like trajectories for both the CoM and the foot swing. The model output can be seen as the optimal trajectory determined based on the average behaviour of 12 healthy subjects walking at three self-selected speeds. Furthermore, the planner formulation is compatible with an extended formulation of the Passive Motion Paradigm which enables us to design a hierarchical architecture of semi-autonomous controllers. The final architecture can also describe the motor primitives as a particular case of dynamic primitives, shows strong parallels with the nervous system organization, and is compatible with the optimal feedback controller theory.

尽管经过数十年的研究，决定人类运动的机制仍然未知，但现有的模型和运动控制理论只能部分地反映这一现象。这可能是下肢康复治疗疗效降低的主要原因。最近有人提出，可以在任务空间中利用作用于质心（CoM）的引力拉来计划人类的运动，我们已经用它来制定用于直线运动的任务空间计划器速度。所提出的模型将CoM横向轨迹表示为以恒定速度向前移动的简谐振子。另一方面，CoM的垂直轨迹通过脚踝策略来控制。我们的解决方案由封闭形式的方程组成，可以为CoM和脚摆动计划类似人类的轨迹。模型输出可被视为基于12个健康受试者以三个自选速度行走的平均行为而确定的最佳轨迹。此外，规划师的公式与被动运动范式的扩展公式相匹配，使我们能够设计出半自主控制器的分层架构。最终的体系结构还可以将电机原语描述为动态原语的特定情况，与神经系统组织显示出强烈的相似性，并且与最优反馈控制器理论兼容。
